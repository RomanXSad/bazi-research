Hard truth: Every paid channel loses money per customer at current amulet price points. The only profitable acquisition is organic (SEO, TikTok, Reddit, viral). This isn't a flaw in execution — it's a structural constraint of selling $25 physical products with 2-5% conversion.
What would make paid acquisition work?
Option A: Increase ASP to $50-75 (premium tier only, better packaging, higher perceived value).
Option B: Subscription add-on ($3-5/mo for daily BaZi pushes) — recurring revenue offsets high CPA.
Option C: Increase conversion (10% amulet view → 25%, 5% purchase → 10%) — but needs UX research.
Option D: Affiliate/wholesale model (sell amulets through existing Feng Shui stores) — different channel entirely.

Recommendations

1. Invest $300 in Google Ads probe test — not to acquire traffic, but to VALIDATE conversion
The probe test answers the question: "Do people who search 'Chinese name generator' actually use our tool and want our amulet?"
If YES → SEO is justified. If NO → pivot before building.
2. Zero paid acquisition until SEO is established
First 3-6 months: build the name generator tool as an SEO asset. Write 20-30 articles around Chinese names, BaZi, zodiac.
Cost: $0-500/mo. Target: 5k-15k organic visits by month 6.
3. Don't spend $30k on marketing — spend it on the product + first inventory
Recommended allocation:
$10k — MVP build (name gen + BaZi + amulet flow + behavior tracker)
$5k — First inventory batch (500-1,000 units, ~$2.5k) + packaging ($1k) + samples ($500)
$5k — SEO content (50+ articles on Fiverr/Upwork, $50-100/article)
$3k — Probe tests across channels ($1k Google Ads + $1k TG Ads + $1k influencers)
$2k — Legal + domain + misc
$5k — Working capital buffer
4. Consider subscription to fix unit economics
Adding a $3-5/mo "daily BaZi push" subscription would dramatically change the model:
At 1% sub conversion of 50k visitors = 500 subs × $4 avg = $2,000/mo recurring.
This makes paid acquisition viable (CAC of $50 recouped in 12-24 months of subscriptions).
