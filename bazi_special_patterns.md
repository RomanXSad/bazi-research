# BaZi Special Patterns & Formations (特殊格局) — Complete Reference

> Regular (regular/regular) patterns follow the standard Five-Element (五行) balance model — suppress the strong, support the weak (抑强扶弱). Special patterns are exceptions where one element dominates so overwhelmingly that the standard balance logic is inverted.

## 1. Overview: Regular vs. Special Patterns

| Dimension | Regular Pattern (正格) | Special Pattern (特殊格局) |
|---|---|---|
| **Logic** | Suppress the strong, support the weak (抑强扶弱) | Follow the strong (从强 / 从旺) or follow the weak (从弱) |
| **Day Master (日主)** | Moderate strength relative to others | Overwhelmingly strong OR overwhelmingly weak |
| **Yong Shen (用神)** | Element that balances the chart | Element that reinforces the dominant trend |
| **Ji Shen (忌神)** | Element that disrupts balance | Element that opposes the dominant trend |
| **Prevalence** | ~70–80% of charts | ~20–30% of charts |
| **Difficulty** | Moderate — standard balance model | High — requires pattern recognition first |

### When to Consider a Special Pattern

Check for a special pattern **BEFORE** applying regular balance logic. The order of precedence:

```
1. Does the Day Master have overwhelming support (same element + producing element)?
2. Is the Day Master overwhelmingly weak with no support?
3. If yes to either → check for special pattern conditions.
4. If no to both → use regular pattern (standard balance).
```

## 2. Zhuan Wang Ge (专旺格) — Special Prosperity Pattern

Zhuan Wang Ge applies when the **Day Master's element** dominates the entire chart. The principle is: **do not suppress what is overwhelmingly strong — instead, support it further.**

The name translates to "Specialized Prosperity Pattern" — the Day Master's element is "specialized" (专) and "prosperous" (旺).

### 2.1 Full / True Zhuan Wang Ge (真专旺格)

#### General Conditions (ALL must be met):

| # | Condition | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day Master element ≥ 80% of the chart's elemental weight | At least 6 of the 8 characters are of the **same element type** or produce it |
| 2 | No element that **overcomes** (克) the Day Master | e.g., no Metal if Day Master is Wood; no Water if Day Master is Fire |
| 3 | No element that **overcomes** the producing (生) element of Day Master | e.g., no Water to overcome Fire if the producer is Wood (Wood produces Fire); actually — check: 忌神 is the element that克 the DM's element |
| 4 | No Official/Authority (官杀) in the Heavenly Stems | The Officer (正官) or Seven Kill (七杀) must NOT appear in any Heavenly Stem |
| 5 | The Season (month branch) strongly supports the Day Master's element | The month branch is the same element as DM or produces it |

#### Sub-types by Element

**A) Qu Ge (曲格 / 曲直格) — Wood Special Prosperity (木专旺)**

Also called 曲直仁寿格 (Curved-Straight Benevolence Longevity Pattern).

| Condition | Detail |
|---|---|
| Day Master | 甲 or 乙 (Wood) |
| Month Branch | 寅 (Tiger/Yin), 卯 (Rabbit/Mao), 辰 (Dragon/Chen) |
| Chart composition | Dominated by 木 (Wood) + 水 (Water produces Wood) |
| Forbidden | 庚 (Geng Metal) or 辛 (Xin Metal) in stems — Metal克Wood |
| Also forbidden | 申 (Shen/Monkey) or 酉 (Rooster/You) in branches — Metal克Wood |

**Example — True Qu Ge:**
```
Year   Month   Day   Hour
乙卯    戊寅    甲寅    乙丑
Wood   Earth   Wood   Earth
(Earth from 戊 and 丑 is weak — only 2 Earth vs. 6 Water+Wood)
```
- Day Master: 甲 (Yang Wood)
- Month: 寅 (Tiger, Yang Wood) — supports Wood
- Chart: 乙卯, 甲寅, 乙 are Wood; 寅 is Wood; 卯 is Wood; 丑 has some Water (癸)
- 戊 (Earth) is weak — only 2 Earth characters
- No Metal克Wood → True Qu Ge

**B) Yan Shang Ge (炎上格 / 炎上格) — Fire Special Prosperity (火专旺)**

Also called 炎上格 (Blazing Flame Pattern).

| Condition | Detail |
|---|---|
| Day Master | 丙 or 丁 (Fire) |
| Month Branch | 巳 (Snake/Si), 午 (Horse/Wu), 未 (Sheep/Wei) |
| Chart composition | Dominated by 火 (Fire) + 木 (Wood produces Fire) |
| Forbidden | 壬 (Ren Water) or 癸 (Gui Water) in stems — Water克Fire |
| Also forbidden | 亥 (Hai/Pig) or 子 (Zi/Rat) in branches — Water克Fire |

**Example — True Yan Shang Ge:**
```
Year   Month   Day   Hour
丙寅    甲午    丙午    乙未
Fire   Wood    Fire   Fire
```
- Day Master: 丙 (Yang Fire)
- Month: 午 (Yang Fire) — peak Fire season
- Chart: 丙, 丙, 午, 未 are Fire; 寅, 甲, 乙 are Wood (produce Fire)
- No Water克Fire → True Yan Shang Ge

**C) Jia Se Ge (稼穡格 / 稼穑格) — Earth Special Prosperity (土专旺)**

Also called 稼穑格 (Planting and Harvesting Pattern).

| Condition | Detail |
|---|---|
| Day Master | 戊 or 己 (Earth) |
| Month Branch | 辰 (Chen), 戌 (Xu), 丑 (Chou), 未 (Wei) — the four Earth months |
| Chart composition | Dominated by 土 (Earth) + 火 (Fire produces Earth) |
| Forbidden | 甲 (Jia Wood) or 乙 (Yi Wood) in stems — Wood克Earth |
| Also forbidden | 寅 (Yin/Tiger) or 卯 (Mao/Rabbit) in branches — Wood克Earth |

**D) Cong Ge (从革格) — Metal Special Prosperity (金专旺)**

Also called 从革格 (Following-Reform Pattern).

| Condition | Detail |
|---|---|
| Day Master | 庚 or 辛 (Metal) |
| Month Branch | 申 (Shen), 酉 (You), 戌 (Xu) — Metal/autumn months |
| Chart composition | Dominated by 金 (Metal) + 土 (Earth produces Metal) |
| Forbidden | 丙 (Bing Fire) or 丁 (Ding Fire) in stems — Fire克Metal |
| Also forbidden | 午 (Wu/Horse) or 巳 (Si/Snake) in branches — Fire克Metal |

**E) Run Xia Ge (润下格) — Water Special Prosperity (水专旺)**

Also called 润下格 (Moistening-Descending Pattern).

| Condition | Detail |
|---|---|
| Day Master | 壬 or 癸 (Water) |
| Month Branch | 亥 (Hai), 子 (Zi), 丑 (Chou) — Water/winter months |
| Chart composition | Dominated by 水 (Water) + 金 (Metal produces Water) |
| Forbidden | 戊 (Wu Earth) or 己 (Ji Earth) in stems — Earth克Water |
| Also forbidden | 未 (Wei/Sheep) or 戌 (Xu/Dog) in branches — Earth克Water |

### 2.2 Fake / Impure Zhuan Wang Ge (假专旺格)

A chart qualifies as **"Fake" Zhuan Wang** when it mostly meets conditions but has **minor violations**:

| Violation Type | Example | How It's "Fake" |
|---|---|---|
| One weak克-element present but hidden | One 庚 Metal in hidden branch (not stem) | The克 element exists but is weak/restrained |
| One克-element in stem but restrained | 庚 (Metal)克甲 (Wood), but 丙 (Fire)克庚 in the same chart | Restrained克-element can't actually克 |
| Month branch slightly off | Wood DM born in 辰 (Chen, Dragon) — Earth season but contains Water | Borderline seasonal support |
| One producing element is weak | Fire DM has 木 producers but they're weak | Still follows fire pattern but less purely |

**Key Principle for Fake patterns:** The Day Master still overwhelmingly dominates, but there is a "blemish" — one opposing element present but too weak to harm. The Yong Shen is still the same element as the DM, but one may need to **temporarily neutralize the impurity** before reinforcing.

## 3. Cong Ge (从格) — Following Patterns

Cong Ge (从格) applies when the Day Master is **overwhelmingly weak** and has **no support** (no same-element stems/branches, no producing element). The principle is: **follow the majority** — align with the dominant element instead of opposing it.

### 3.1 Cong Wang Ge (从旺格) — Following Strength

The Day Master is weak, but the **dominant element** is the **same as** the Day Master's own element. Wait — this is a contradiction. Let me clarify the actual classification:

**Correct classification:**

| Pattern | DM Strength | Dominant Element | Logic |
|---|---|---|---|
| Zhuan Wang Ge | Overwhelmingly STRONG | DM's element | Reinforce DM |
| Cong Wang Ge | Overwhelmingly STRONG | DM's element | **Same as Zhuan Wang** (some schools merge these) |
| Cong Ruo Ge | Overwhelmingly WEAK | NOT DM's element | Follow the dominant element |
| Cong Qiang Ge | Overwhelmingly WEAK | Producing element of DM (印星) | Follow the 印 (resource) |

**Clarification:** Classical BaZi divides this as:

- **Zhuan Wang Ge** = DM is overwhelmingly strong (dominant).
- **Cong Ge** = DM is overwhelmingly weak (submissive). **Cong = to follow/submit.**

**Cong Wang Ge = Cong Qiang Ge** (从旺格 / 从强格): DM is weak, but the dominant elements are the **印 (Resource/Producer)** and the **Bi Jie (Peer/Same element)**. The chart is full of the DM's element and its producer. So the DM follows the strength of its own group.

Wait — let me re-check this carefully from classical theory.

#### Actually Correct Classification:

**Cong Ge (从格)** refers to the Day Master **submitting to / following** the dominant element. There are two main branches:

1. **Cong Qiang (从强)** — DM submits to strength of its own element group (印 + 比劫 dominate). This is essentially Zhuan Wang-like but the DM isn't itself overwhelmingly present — its supporters are.
2. **Cong Ruo (从弱)** — DM submits to the opposite element.

But **many modern schools** simplify this:

#### Modern Simplified Classification:

```
Cong Ge (从格)
├── Cong Wang (从旺) = Zhuan Wang Ge (专旺格) — DM very strong
│   └── 5 sub-types (Wood/Fire/Earth/Metal/Water)
│
└── Cong Ruo (从弱) — DM very weak, follows the strongest element
    ├── Cong Guan / Sha (从官/杀) — Follow Officer / Seven Kill
    ├── Cong Cai (从财) — Follow Wealth
    ├── Cong Shi Shen (从食伤) — Follow Output (Food/Resource)
    └── Cong Shi (从势) — Follow the general momentum (mixed)
```

I will document the **most widely accepted** classification:

### 3.2 Cong Ruo Ge (从弱格) — Following Weakness (DM is Weak)

The Day Master is overwhelmingly weak with **no support at all** — no same-element stems/branches, no producing (印) element. The DM must "surrender" to the dominant force.

#### Conditions for Cong Ruo Ge (ALL must be met):

| # | Condition | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day Master element ≤ 20% of chart | At most 1 character of DM's element and none producing it |
| 2 | No 印 (Resource/Producer) in stems | The element that produces the DM must NOT appear in Heavenly Stems |
| 3 | No Bi Jie (比劫/Peer) in stems | No same-element character in Heavenly Stems (except possibly hidden/weak in branches) |
| 4 | The dominant element is opposite or controlling | The chart's majority is the克 DM,耗 DM, or泄 DM element |
| 5 | The season does NOT support DM | Month branch produces克或泄 of DM, not DM itself |

### 3.3 Sub-types of Cong Ruo Ge

#### A) Cong Guan / Sha Ge (从官杀格) — Follow Officer / Seven Kill

The dominant element is the **element that克 (controls)** the Day Master.

| Day Master | Dominant Element | Controlling Element |
|---|---|---|
| 甲/乙 (Wood) | 金 (Metal) | 庚/辛 — Metal克Wood |
| 丙/丁 (Fire) | 水 (Water) | 壬/癸 — Water克Fire |
| 戊/己 (Earth) | 木 (Wood) | 甲/乙 — Wood克Earth |
| 庚/辛 (Metal) | 火 (Fire) | 丙/丁 — Fire克Metal |
| 壬/癸 (Water) | 土 (Earth) | 戊/己 — Earth克Water |

**Yong Shen:** The controlling element (官杀) itself. Also support elements that strengthen the controlling element.

**Example — Cong Guan Ge:**
```
Year   Month   Day   Hour
庚申    丙戌    甲午    辛未
Metal  Fire    Wood   Metal
```
- Day Master: 甲 (Yang Wood)
- Chart: 庚, 申, 辛 are Metal (克 Wood); 丙, 午 are Fire (泄 Wood); 戌, 未 are Earth (耗 Wood)
- Wood support: ZERO — no 寅, 卯, 乙, 甲 except Day Master itself
- 甲 has NO supporters — it sits on 午 (Fire, draining), surrounded by Metal克
- **Pattern: Cong Sha Ge** (从杀格 — follows Seven Kill)
- **Yong Shen:** 金 (Metal), 土 (Earth produces Metal)
- **Ji Shen:** 水 (Water泄 Metal), 木 (Wood resists Metal)

#### B) Cong Cai Ge (从财格) — Follow Wealth

The dominant element is the **element that the DM克 (controls)** — i.e., Wealth (财).

| Day Master | Dominant Element | Wealth Element |
|---|---|---|
| 甲/乙 (Wood) | 土 (Earth) | 戊/己/辰/戌/丑/未 |
| 丙/丁 (Fire) | 金 (Metal) | 庚/辛/申/酉 |
| 戊/己 (Earth) | 水 (Water) | 壬/癸/亥/子 |
| 庚/辛 (Metal) | 木 (Wood) | 甲/乙/寅/卯 |
| 壬/癸 (Water) | 火 (Fire) | 丙/丁/巳/午 |

**Yong Shen:** The Wealth element (财) itself. Also its producer (食伤 — the element that produces the wealth element).

**Example — Cong Cai Ge:**
```
Year   Month   Day   Hour
戊辰    壬戌    甲申    己巳
Earth  Water   Wood   Earth
```
- Day Master: 甲 (Yang Wood)
- Chart: 戊, 辰, 戌, 己 are Earth (财 for Wood); 申 is Metal (克 Wood); 巳 is Fire (泄 Wood); 壬 is Water (producer but weak)
- Earth dominates — 5 Earth characters
- Wood has NO supporters (no 寅, 卯, 乙)
- DM (甲) sits on 申 (Metal克 it) — no resistance possible
- **Pattern: Cong Cai Ge** (从财格 — follows Wealth)
- **Yong Shen:** 土 (Earth — Wealth), 火 (Fire produces Earth)
- **Ji Shen:** 水 (Water泄 Fire, 克 Fire), 木 (Wood — resists Earth)

#### C) Cong Shi Shen Ge (从食伤格) — Follow Output

The dominant element is the **element that the DM produces** (泄 — drains the DM) — i.e., Food/Output (食伤).

| Day Master | Dominant Element | Output Element |
|---|---|---|
| 甲/乙 (Wood) | 火 (Fire) | 丙/丁/巳/午 |
| 丙/丁 (Fire) | 土 (Earth) | 戊/己/辰/戌/丑/未 |
| 戊/己 (Earth) | 金 (Metal) | 庚/辛/申/酉 |
| 庚/辛 (Metal) | 水 (Water) | 壬/癸/亥/子 |
| 壬/癸 (Water) | 木 (Wood) | 甲/乙/寅/卯 |

**Yong Shen:** The Output element (食伤) itself. Also Wealth (财 — the element the Output produces).

**Example — Cong Shi Shen Ge:**
```
Year   Month   Day   Hour
丙午    甲午    戊午    丁巳
Fire   Wood    Earth  Fire
```
- Day Master: 戊 (Yang Earth)
- Chart: 丙, 午, 午, 丁, 巳 are Fire (印 for Earth — produces Earth); 甲 is Wood (克 Earth); 戊 is Earth
- Actually this has strong印 (Fire producing Earth) — this might be Zhuan Wang (Earth) or Cong Qiang
- Better example:

```
Year   Month   Day   Hour
丙午    甲午    甲午    丁卯
Fire   Wood    Wood   Fire
```
- Day Master: 甲 (Yang Wood)
- Chart: 丙, 午, 午, 丁, 卯 are Fire/卯is Wood; 甲, 甲 are Wood
- Fire dominates (5 Fire vs. 3 Wood)
- DM is Wood producing Fire — the Fire drains Wood completely
- No Water to support Wood
- **Pattern: Cong Shi Shen Ge** (从食伤格 — follows Output/Fire)
- **Yong Shen:** 火 (Fire — Output/食伤), 土 (Earth — Wealth produced by Fire)
- **Ji Shen:** 水 (Water克 Fire), 金 (Metal耗 Fire)

#### D) Cong Shi Ge (从势格) — Following the Momentum

When no single element dominates, but the DM is still overwhelmingly weak, and the resisting elements (克, 泄, 耗) are all present and mixed. The name "从势" means "follow the general momentum/power."

**Condition:** The chart has a mix of克, 泄, 耗 elements, none clearly dominant, but ALL oppose the DM. No single element ≥50%, but DM's supporters are ≤1 character.

**Yong Shen:** The **strongest** opposing element. Or in some interpretations, the element that best "fits" the momentum (often the one most abundant).

**Ji Shen:** Any element that supports the DM (印 and 比劫).

### 3.4 Comparison: Cong Ge vs. Zhuan Wang Ge

| Aspect | Zhuan Wang Ge (专旺格) | Cong Ge (从格) |
|---|---|---|
| DM Strength | Overwhelmingly STRONG | Overwhelmingly WEAK |
| Dominant Element | DM's element | Opposite / controlling / draining |
| Yong Shen | DM's element + its producer | The dominant element + its supporter |
| Ji Shen | The克-DM element | Any DM-supporter |
| Life implication | Strong personality, can handle pressure | Adaptive, flexible, follower |
| Risk | If broken, sudden collapse | If broken, chaos and instability |

## 4. Jia Ge (假格) — Fake / Impure Patterns

Jia Ge (假格) refers to special patterns that **almost** qualify but have minor impurities. They are not full special patterns, but they're not regular patterns either.

### 4.1 Types of Jia Ge

#### A) Jia Zhuan Wang Ge (假专旺格) — Fake Special Prosperity

The DM is very strong (approaching Zhuan Wang), but:

- One克-element exists but is very weak (e.g., hidden in a branch, restrained by another element)
- OR one泄-element leaks energy but doesn't break the pattern
- OR the season is not perfectly aligned

**Example:**
```
Year   Month   Day   Hour
癸卯    乙卯    甲寅    辛未
Water  Wood    Wood   Metal
```
- Day Master: 甲 (Yang Wood)
- Chart: 卯, 乙, 卯, 寅, 甲 are Wood (5 Wood); 癸 is Water (producer); 辛 is Metal (克Wood); 未 is Earth
- **Almost Qu Ge** — but has 辛 (Xin Metal) in Hour Stem
- 辛 is Metal克Wood — a "blemish"
- However, 辛 sits on 未 (Earth耗Metal, Fire库 — weakens Metal) and is alone
- **Pattern: Fake Qu Ge (假曲格)**
- **Yong Shen:** Still 木 (Wood) + 水 (Water) — but must be cautious with 辛
- The 辛 is so weak it cannot actually harm — but it's still present

**Diagnosis:** If the克-element is restrained, concealed, or has no root (no supportive branch), the pattern is FAKE but functional.

#### B) Jia Cong Ge (假从格) — Fake Following Pattern

The DM is very weak (approaching Cong Ruo), but:

- One印 (producer) exists but is very weak (no root, hidden)
- OR one Bi Jie (peer) exists but is weak and restrained
- OR the DM has a root (branch) but it's damaged

**Example:**
```
Year   Month   Day   Hour
壬申    丙午    甲寅    己巳
Water  Fire    Wood   Earth
```
- Day Master: 甲 (Yang Wood)
- Chart looks like mostly Fire + Earth — DM is weak
- BUT: 甲 sits on 寅 (Tiger — Wood root), and 壬 (Water) produces Wood
- 甲 has ONE root (寅) and ONE producer (壬) — weak but present
- **Pattern: Fake Cong Ge (假从格)** — nearly follows but has weak support
- Cannot be treated as pure Cong Ge

**Diagnosis:** The pattern is "fake" — you can lean towards Cong Ge treatment but must handle with care. In lucky periods that strengthen the DM's weak supporters, the person may behave more like a regular pattern.

### 4.2 How to Handle Jia Ge in Practice

| Scenario | Approach |
|---|---|
| One weak克-element in Zhuan Wang | Treat as Zhuan Wang but that克-element's luck pillar periods are dangerous |
| Hidden克-element in branch | Treat as Zhuan Wang — hidden branch elements matter less for day-to-day |
| Weak印 in Cong Ruo | Treat as Cong Ruo but印 periods bring conflict (momentary resistance) |
| One weak Bi Jie in Cong Ruo | Treat as Cong Ruo — the single peer can't help but creates internal tension |

**Goldman Rule for Jia Ge:** "The presence of an impurity does not change the pattern — it changes how the pattern behaves in different luck pillars."

## 5. Elemental Balance Diagnosis: Cold / Hot / Dry / Wet

This is critical for determining the chart's **temperature and moisture**, which affects which element is truly needed as Yong Shen — even in special patterns.

### 5.1 The Four Conditions

| Condition | Characteristics | Related Element |
|---|---|---|
| **Cold (寒)** | Winter-born; lots of Water (壬/癸/亥/子); Metal abundant (produces Water) | Water excess |
| **Hot (暖/热)** | Summer-born; lots of Fire (丙/丁/巳/午); Wood abundant (produces Fire) | Fire excess |
| **Dry (燥)** | Abundant Earth (especially 戌/Dog, 未/Sheep — dry Earth); lack of Water | Earth excess (dry Earth) |
| **Wet (湿)** | Abundant Water (亥/子) or Wet Earth (辰/Dragon, 丑/Ox); lack of Fire | Water + Wet Earth |

### 5.2 Diagnosis Method

#### Step 1: Assess the Season (Month Branch)

| Month | Season | Inherent Qi |
|---|---|---|
| 寅 (Tiger, Feb) | Spring | Warming — from cold to hot |
| 卯 (Rabbit, Mar) | Spring | Warm, Wood dominant |
| 辰 (Dragon, Apr) | Spring→Summer transition | Warm, wet (Water reservoir) |
| 巳 (Snake, May) | Summer | Hot, Fire begins |
| 午 (Horse, Jun) | Summer | Peak hot |
| 未 (Sheep, Jul) | Summer→Autumn transition | Hot, dry (Fire reservoir) |
| 申 (Monkey, Aug) | Autumn | Cooling, Metal begins |
| 酉 (Rooster, Sep) | Autumn | Dry, Metal peak |
| 戌 (Dog, Oct) | Autumn→Winter transition | Cooling, dry (Metal reservoir) |
| 亥 (Pig, Nov) | Winter | Cold, Water begins |
| 子 (Rat, Dec) | Winter | Peak cold |
| 丑 (Ox, Jan) | Winter→Spring transition | Cold, wet (Water reservoir) |

#### Step 2: Check the Heavenly Stems for Dominant Climate

- 丙/丁 (Fire) in stems → Hot
- 壬/癸 (Water) in stems → Cold
- 戊/己 on dry Earth branches (戌/未/辰) → Dry
- 壬/癸 on wet branches (亥/子/丑/辰) → Wet

#### Step 3: Check the Earth Branches for Hidden Climates

| Branch | Hidden Stems | Climate |
|---|---|---|
| 辰 (Chen/Dragon) | 戊, 乙, 癸 | Wet (Water reservoir) |
| 戌 (Xu/Dog) | 戊, 辛, 丁 | Dry (Fire reservoir) |
| 丑 (Chou/Ox) | 己, 癸, 辛 | Wet (Water reservoir) |
| 未 (Wei/Sheep) | 己, 丁, 乙 | Dry (Fire reservoir) |
| 亥 (Hai/Pig) | 壬, 甲 | Cold, wet |
| 子 (Zi/Rat) | 癸 | Cold, wet |
| 巳 (Si/Snake) | 丙, 戊, 庚 | Hot, dry |
| 午 (Wu/Horse) | 丁, 己 | Hot, dry |

#### Step 4: Synthesize — Determine the Net Climate

```
Net Climate = Season + Fire/Water presence + Dry/Wet Earth presence

Examples:
- Winter (子/丑) + 壬水 + 亥水 = Cold + Wet → "Very Cold and Wet"
- Summer (午/未) + 丙火 + 未土 = Hot + Dry → "Very Hot and Dry"
- Spring (寅/卯) + 甲木 + 辰土 = Warm + Wet → "Warm and slightly Wet"
- Autumn (申/酉) + 庚金 + 戌土 = Cool + Dry → "Cool and Dry"
```

### 5.3 The "Diao Hou" (调候) Principle — Climate Adjustment

**调候 (Diao Hou) = Climate Regulation / Seasonal Adjustment.**

This is a fundamental BaZi principle: **every chart needs climate balance regardless of pattern type.** Even a Zhuan Wang Ge needs climate consideration.

| Climate | Problem | Solution (调候用神) |
|---|---|---|
| Too Cold (过寒) | Stagnation, freezing, depression | Add Fire (丙/丁/午/巳) — warmth thaws |
| Too Hot (过热) | Agitation, burnout, volatility | Add Water (壬/癸/亥/子) — cooling soothes |
| Too Dry (过燥) | Rigidity, conflict, dryness | Add Water (moisture) |
| Too Wet (过湿) | Passivity, stagnation, heaviness | Add Fire (drying warmth) |

#### The 10-Day Master Climate Reference Table

| Day Master | Born in | Climate Need | Preferred Yong Shen |

```
Month: 壬戌 → 壬 (Yang Water, Stem), 戌 (Yang Earth, Branch — 戊辛丁)
Day: 甲午   → 甲 (Yang Wood, Stem), 午 (Yang Fire, Branch — 丁己)
Hour: 丙寅  → 丙 (Yang Fire, Stem), 寅 (Yang Wood, Branch — 甲丙戊)
```

**Element Distribution:**
| Element | Count | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 火 (Fire) | 3 | 丙, 午, (丁 in 午+戌) |
| 土 (Earth) | 3+ | 戊, 戌, (己+戊 hidden) |
| 金 (Metal) | 2 | 申, (辛 in 戌) |
| 水 (Water) | 2 | 壬, (壬 in 申) |
| 木 (Wood) | 2 | 甲, 寅 |

**Pattern Diagnosis:**
- Day Master: 甲 (Yang Wood)
- 甲 has ONE supporter: 寅 (Wood root) — but 寅 is in Hour, not month
- Overall: all elements oppose or drain Wood
- No single element dominates — all five elements are present
- **= CONG SHI GE (从势格)** — follow the general momentum

**Yong Shen:** The strongest opposition. Here, 火 (Fire) and 土 (Earth) are roughly equal. Given that 丙 appears in Hour Stem and 午 is the Day Branch, 火 might be slightly stronger. But 土 has 戊 in Year + 戌 in Month.

In Cong Shi, the Yong Shen is the dominant force. This will depend on qualitative analysis. If 土 is strongest → treat as Cong Cai (从财). If 火 is strongest → Cong Shi Shen (从食伤). If truly mixed → 土 (cai) as primary because 财 is often prioritized for career.

**Ji Shen:** 木 (Wood — DM's element), 水 (Water produces Wood)
**Xi Shen:** Depends on which element dominates

**Life Interpretation:**
- Extremely adaptable — can succeed in many environments
- Jack of all trades, master of many
- Needs to "go with the flow" — resistance leads to failure
- Success depends on correctly reading which energy to follow

### 9.4 Common Mistakes & Pitfalls

| Mistake | Correction |
|---|---|
| Treating a strong regular chart as Zhuan Wang | Zhuan Wang requires ≥80% of one element, not just "strong" |
| Treating a weak regular chart as Cong Ge | Cong Ge requires ZERO effective support for DM |
| Ignoring climate in special patterns | Climate can override pattern logic |
| Forgetting hidden stems | Hidden stems in branches may contain the "forbidden" element |
| Treating Fake patterns same as True | Fake patterns have different reactions to Luck Pillars |
| Assuming Cong Ge means "always follow" | Even Cong Ge charts have periods where resisting works |
