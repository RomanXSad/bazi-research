## Part I: Seasonal Element Power Table — 旺相休囚死 (Wàng Xiāng Xiū Qiú Sǐ)

### 1.1 What Is 旺相休囚死?

The **Wàng Xiāng Xiū Qiú Sǐ** system describes the five **dynamic states** an element experiences as it moves through the four seasons. It is the most fundamental strength-assessment framework in BaZi — more foundational than the 12-Stage Life Cycle (十二长生). Every element in a chart is assigned one of five seasonal power levels in each season:

| Chinese | Pinyin | Translation | Meaning (Strength Level) |
|---------|--------|-------------|--------------------------|
| 旺 | Wàng | Flourishing / Prosperous | **Peak power** — the element that rules the season |
| 相 | Xiāng | Mutual / Prime | **Supported / Nourished** — the element that is "born" by the ruling element |
| 休 | Xiū | Rest / Retreat | **Resting / Retired** — the element that "gave birth" to the ruler |
| 囚 | Qiú | Imprisoned / Trapped | **Weakened / Contained** — the element that is controlled by the ruler |
| 死 | Sǐ | Dead / Perished | **Minimal / Vanquished** — the element that controls the ruler |

> **Mnemonic sequence** (the Cycle of States):
> 旺 → 相 → 休 → 囚 → 死
>
> The order follows the **Generating Cycle (相生)** forward from 旺, then the **Controlling Cycle (相克)**.

> **\* Transition (季月 / Four Corners):** The last 18 days of each season are considered "Earth seasons" — the transitional periods when Earth governs. Specifically, the last ~18 days of each of the four seasons (late Spring = 辰, late Summer = 未, late Autumn = 戌, late Winter = 丑). In simplified frameworks, these four "Earth months" (辰/未/戌/丑) are grouped together.

   - Spring is the time of growth → **Wood** Wàng.
   - Summer is the time of heat/peak activity → **Fire** Wàng.
   - Autumn is the time of harvest/contraction → **Metal** Wàng.
   - Winter is the time of stillness/storage → **Water** Wàng.
   - Transition periods are times of change/grounding → **Earth** Wàng.

### 1.6 The "Five Element in Each Season" Formula (五行在四季)

This formula, also called **五行四季旺衰**, provides a numeric/ratio framework for how strong each element is in each season. It is expressed as:

> **The formula:** For any given season, the strength of each of the five elements is proportional to its state position on a scale.

Assign numerical values to each state:

| State | Strength Value |
|-------|---------------|

| 相 Xiāng | **4** (high / nourished) |
| 休 Xiū | **3** (moderate / resting) |

| **Winter** | 4 (相) | 1 (死) | 2 (囚) | 3 (休) | 5 (旺) |

#### The Cyclic Pattern

The table follows a repeating **diagonal pattern** — shift one column to the right and one row down to follow the Wàng element through the seasons:

```
     Wood  Fire  Earth  Metal  Water
Spr:   W     X     S      Q     Xi
Sum:  Xi     W     X      S     Q
Aut:   Q    Xi     W      X     S
Win:   S     Q    Xi      W     X
```

Where: W = 旺 (5), X = 相 (4), Xi = 休 (3), Q = 囚 (2), S = 死 (1)

#### Practical Application

This numerical model allows BaZi practitioners to:

1. **Score each element** in a chart based on which season it's born in
2. **Compare pillar strength** — an element at 相 (4) near a supporting stem might outrank a Wàng element with no support
3. **Calculate elemental balance** — sum all element scores from pillars + hidden stems + seasonal modifier
4. **Determine 用神 (Useful God)** — the element that restores balance to the chart by either supporting the weak or controlling the excessive

## Part II: Na Yin (纳音) Tones

### 2.1 What Are Na Yin Tones?

**Na Yin (纳音)** translates literally to "**assigned sound**" or "**received tone**." In BaZi, every **Stem-Branch combination** (干支组合) — i.e., each of the 60 Sexagenary pairs (六十甲子) — is assigned a specific Na Yin element. There are **30 unique Na Yin tones** covering all 60 Stem-Branch pairs (each tone covers two consecutive pairs).

Unlike the **Element of the Heavenly Stem** (天干五行) or the **Element of the Earthly Branch** (地支五行), the Na Yin represents the **"essence" or "quality"** of the Stem-Branch pair as a whole — a deeper, more subtle layer of elemental influence.

Historically, Na Yin originated from **ancient Chinese music theory** (五音 / Five Tones): 宫 (Gong), 商 (Shang), 角 (Jue), 徵 (Zhi), 羽 (Yu) — corresponding to Earth, Metal, Wood, Fire, Water respectively. The "tone" (音) of a Stem-Branch pair was believed to resonate with a specific musical note of the universe.

### 2.3 How Na Yin Is Derived

#### Step 1: Obtain the Sexagenary Pair Index

Each year, month, day, and hour pillar is a Stem-Branch pair. The pair maps to an index 0–59 in the Sexagenary cycle (六十甲子).

#### Step 2: The Calculation Method

There are three derivation methods historically used:

**Method A: The Classical Formula (干支纳音)**

The Na Yin element is derived from the **combination of the Stem's and Branch's Yin-Yang position** along with their Five Element correspondence. The formula follows:

1. Take the **Heavenly Stem number** (1-10) and **Earthly Branch number** (1-12)
2. Apply: `(Stem_Number + Branch_Number) mod 5` → maps to one of the Five Elements
3. Further refined by the **pair's position parity** (odd/even index in the cycle)

However, this algebraic method is cumbersome. In practice, practitioners simply use the memorized table or a lookup.

**Method B: The Sexagenary Pair Index Method**

```
index = (stem_number - 1) * 6 + (branch_number - 1)  // 0-indexed
na_yin_index = floor(index / 2)  // 0 to 29
```

Where:
- Stem numbering: 甲=1, 乙=2, ... 癸=10
- Branch numbering: 子=1, 丑=2, ... 亥=12

**Method C: The Gua (Trigram) Method**

A more esoteric method using the 8 Trigrams (八卦):
- Assign the 5 elements to the 5 tones: 宫(Earth)=1, 商(Metal)=2, 角(Wood)=3, 徵(Fire)=4, 羽(Water)=5
- Use the Lie (列) formula involving the Stem and Branch numbers in a specific algorithm involving subtraction and modulo operations with the number 5
- Result is the **tone element** of the pair

### 2.4 The Role of Na Yin in BaZi Reading

Na Yin is considered a **secondary but important layer** of analysis, used in the following ways:

#### 1. Personality Sub-Type (气质细分)

The Na Yin adds nuance to the Day Master's elemental type. Two Wood Day Masters born under different Na Yin are subtly different:

| Na Yin | Personality Nuance |
|--------|-------------------|
| 松柏木 (Pine & Cypress Wood) | Resilient, principled, stands tall against adversity |
| 杨柳木 (Willow Wood) | Flexible, artistic, goes with the flow |
| 石榴木 (Pomegranate Wood) | Productive, fertile, cyclical nature |
| 大林木 (Great Forest Wood) | Communal, broad, collective mindset |
| 平地木 (Flat Plain Wood) | Ordinary, grounded, unpretentious |

#### 2. Element Interaction with the Year Pillar

The **Na Yin of the Year Pillar** (年柱纳音) is particularly important because it represents the **"Mandate of Heaven"** (天命) — the inherited life circumstances and family background.

- The Year Na Yin's element interacts with the Day Master's element via the Five Element cycles
- If the Year Na Yin **generates** (生) the Day Master → supportive family background
- If the Year Na Yin **controls** (克) the Day Master → challenging early life circumstances

#### 3. Pair Compatibility Analysis

Two Stem-Branch pairs with the **same Na Yin** are considered to have a special affinity. This is used in:

- **Marriage compatibility** — comparing the Year Na Yin of two individuals
- **Business partnership** — shared Na Yin suggests natural resonance
- **Year-on-year influence** — years sharing your Na Yin are considered especially significant

#### 4. Favorable / Unfavorable Years

When the current year's Na Yin interacts with the Day Master's Na Yin:

| Interaction | Effect |
|-------------|--------|
| Same Na Yin | Amplification of the Year's energy |
| Generating cycle | Harmonious, supportive year |
| Controlling cycle | Challenging, growth-oriented year |

#### 5. The Five Element Configuration (五行格局)

In advanced BaZi, the Na Yin of **all four pillars** is analyzed together to form a "Na Yin configuration." For example:

- A chart where all four pillars have **Water Na Yin** → "Water World" configuration (extremely introspective, intuitive)
- A chart mixing **Fire Na Yin + Wood Na Yin** → "Wood fuels Fire" (dynamic, creative life path)
