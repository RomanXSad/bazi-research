## 1. Five Elements (Wu Xing / 五行)

### The 5 Elements

| Element | Chinese | Color | Season | Direction | Nature |
|---------|---------|-------|--------|-----------|--------|
| Wood | 木 | Green | Spring | East | Expansion, creativity, growth |
| Fire | 火 | Red | Summer | South | Passion, energy, transformation |
| Earth | 土 | Yellow | Center (transitions) | Center | Grounding, stability, pivot |
| Metal | 金 | White | Autumn | West | Structure, discipline, refinement |
| Water | 水 | Black | Winter | North | Wisdom, adaptability, depth |

### Cycles

**Generation Cycle (相生)** — each element produces the next:
```
Wood → Fire → Earth → Metal → Water → Wood
```
- Wood feeds Fire
- Fire creates Earth (ash)
- Earth bears Metal (minerals)
- Metal collects Water (condensation)
- Water nourishes Wood

**Destruction/Control Cycle (相克)** — each element controls another:
```
Wood → Earth → Water → Fire → Metal → Wood
```
- Wood breaks Earth (roots)
- Earth dams Water
- Water extinguishes Fire
- Fire melts Metal
- Metal cuts Wood

This is key for reading chart dynamics — a combined element can fundamentally alter the chart's balance.

### Branch Interactions

#### Triple Combinations (三合 / San He)
Three branches form a strong elemental "bureau":

| Combination | Element Bureau |
|-------------|---------------|
| 申+子+辰 (Shēn + Zǐ + Chén) | Water |
| 巳+酉+丑 (Sì + Yǒu + Chǒu) | Metal |
| 寅+午+戌 (Yín + Wǔ + Xū) | Fire |
| 亥+卯+未 (Hài + Mǎo + Wèi) | Wood |

Stronger energy than single branches. Transforms the elemental quality entirely.

#### Six Combinations (六合 / Liu He)
Pairs forming a harmonious bond:

| Pair | Notes |
|------|-------|
| 子+丑 (Zǐ + Chǒu) | |
| 寅+亥 (Yín + Hài) | |
| 卯+戌 (Mǎo + Xū) | |
| 辰+酉 (Chén + Yǒu) | |
| 巳+申 (Sì + Shēn) | |
| 午+未 (Wǔ + Wèi) | |

Softens tension, produces blended elemental effect.

#### Six Clashes (六冲 / Liu Chong)
Directly opposing — disruption, change, conflict:

| Clash | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 子午 (Zǐ vs Wǔ) | |
| 丑未 (Chǒu vs Wèi) | |

| 辰戌 (Chén vs Xū) | |
| 巳亥 (Sì vs Hài) | |

Clashes signal instability in the corresponding life area. Can trigger major events when activated by Luck or Annual Pillar.

#### Three Punishments (三刑 / San Xing)

| Punishment | Type | Meaning |
|-----------|------|---------|
| 寅巳申 (Yín + Sì + Shēn) | Ungrateful (无恩之刑) | Cruelty, ingratitude |

Signals stress or moral conflicts.

| Harm Pair |

#### Three-Direction Meetings (三会 / San Hui)
Strongest elemental concentration in BaZi — three consecutive-direction branches:

| Meeting | Direction | Element |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| 寅卯辰 (Yín + Mǎo + Chén) | East | Wood |
| 巳午未 (Sì + Wǔ + Wèi) | South | Fire |
| 申酉戌 (Shēn + Yǒu + Xū) | West | Metal |
| 亥子丑 (Hài + Zǐ + Chǒu) | North | Water |

Stronger than Triple Combinations. When present, that element dominates the chart.

## 5. Seasons & Five Elements

The Earthly Branches encode the four seasons. The 3rd, 6th, 9th, 12th branches (辰/未/戌/丑) are Earth transition months:

| Season | Branches | Element |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Spring (春) | 寅 (Yín), 卯 (Mǎo), 辰 (Chén) | Wood — 辰 = Earth transition |
| Summer (夏) | 巳 (Sì), 午 (Wǔ), 未 (Wèi) | Fire — 未 = Earth transition |
| Autumn (秋) | 申 (Shēn), 酉 (Yǒu), 戌 (Xū) | Metal — 戌 = Earth transition |
| Winter (冬) | 亥 (Hài), 子 (Zǐ), 丑 (Chǒu) | Water — 丑 = Earth transition |

## 6. Twelve Life Phases (十二长生绝 / Shi Er Chang Sheng)

Every element passes through 12 stages of vitality:

| # | Phase | Chinese | Meaning |
|---|-------|---------|---------|
| 1 | Birth | 长生 | Chang Sheng — new life, growth |
| 2 | Bathing | 沐浴 | Mu Yu — innocence, vulnerability |
| 3 | Dressing | 冠带 | Guan Dai — coming of age |
| 4 | Promotion | 临官 | Lin Guan — official position, prime |
| 5 | Peak | 帝旺 | Di Wang — emperor, maximum power |
| 6 | Decline | 衰 | Shuai — decline begins |
| 7 | Illness | 病 | Bing — sickness, weakness |
| 8 | Death | 死 | Si — death, end |
| 9 | Burial | 墓 | Mu — grave, storage |
| 10 | Void | 绝 | Jue — extinction, womb |
| 11 | Conception | 胎 | Tai — fetus, new potential |
| 12 | Nurturing | 养 | Yang — development, nurture |

These indicate how "strong" an element is at a given chart position. A stem in its "Peak" phase is powerful; in "Death" or "Void" it's weak.

## 7. Ten Gods (十神 / Shi Shen)

The Ten Gods define relationships between the Day Master (日主 — "you" in the chart) and every other stem:

| Relationship | Positive (正/Zheng) | Negative (偏/Pian) | Represents |
|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|------------|
| Produces me (生我者) | 正印 Direct Resource | 偏印 Indirect Resource | Nurturing, support, education, mother |
| I produce (我生者) | 伤官 Hurting Officer | 食神 Eating God | Creativity, expression, children |
| Controls me (克我者) | 正官 Direct Officer | 偏官 Seven Killings | Authority, discipline, career, law |
| I control (我克者) | 正财 Direct Wealth | 偏财 Indirect Wealth | Money, material, partner, father |
| Same as me (同我者) | 比肩 Friend/Rob Wealth | 劫财 Friend/Shoulder | Peers, competition, siblings |

**Key**: The Ten Gods are derived from the Five Element cycle relative to the Day Master stem.

## 8. Sixty-Year Cycle & Void Branches (六十甲子空亡)

## 9. Application Flow (运用篇)

1. **Identify the Day Master** (日主) — the Heavenly Stem of the Day Pillar. This is "you" in the chart.
2. **Assess Day Master strength** — using season, hidden stems, and Life Phases.
3. **Map all stems & branches** to their Ten Gods relationships with the Day Master.
4. **Read Luck Pillars** (大运) & Annual Pillars (流年) for timing of events.
5. **Interpret interactions** — combinations, clashes, punishments, harms, void branches.
