## 1. Step-by-Step Chart Reading Flow

This section provides the **master workflow** — from raw Four Pillars to meaningful life insights. Follow these steps in order for every chart.

### Step 1: Lay Out the Four Pillars (四柱)

Represent the birth moment as a 2×4 grid:

| | Year (年) | Month (月) | Day (日) | Hour (时) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Heavenly Stem (天干)** | 甲/乙/... | 丙/丁/... | 戊/己/... | 庚/辛/... |
| **Earthly Branch (地支)** | 子/丑/... | 寅/卯/... | 辰/巳/... | 午/未/... |

Each stem-branch pair is called a **pillar (柱)**. There are four pillars, hence "Four Pillars" (四柱).

**Example chart:**
```
       Year   Month   Day   Hour
Stem:  甲     丙      戊     庚
Brch:  子     寅      午     申
```

### Step 2: Identify the Day Master (日主)

The **Day Master** is the Heavenly Stem of the Day Pillar. This is "you" — the self at the center of the chart.

| Yang Stems | Yin Stems | Element |
|---|---|---|
| 甲 (Jiǎ) | 乙 (Yǐ) | **Wood** 🌿 |
| 丙 (Bǐng) | 丁 (Dīng) | **Fire** 🔥 |
| 戊 (Wù) | 己 (Jǐ) | **Earth** ⛰️ |
| 庚 (Gēng) | 辛 (Xīn) | **Metal** ⚔️ |
| 壬 (Rén) | 癸 (Guǐ) | **Water** 💧 |

**Rule of thumb:** Yang stems are assertive, forceful, outward. Yin stems are receptive, nuanced, inward. Both can achieve great things, but through different approaches.

### Step 3: Determine the Season (Month Branch)

The **Month Branch** tells us the season of birth, which is the most important factor for elemental strength.

| Branch | Month (approx) | Season |
|---|---|---|
| 寅 (Yín) | Feb 4–Mar 5 | **Spring** (Wood) |
| 卯 (Mǎo) | Mar 6–Apr 4 | **Spring** (Wood) |
| 辰 (Chén) | Apr 5–May 5 | **Spring→Summer** (Earth transition) |
| 巳 (Sì) | May 6–Jun 5 | **Summer** (Fire) |
| 午 (Wǔ) | Jun 6–Jul 6 | **Summer** (Fire) |
| 未 (Wèi) | Jul 7–Aug 7 | **Summer→Autumn** (Earth transition) |
| 申 (Shēn) | Aug 8–Sep 7 | **Autumn** (Metal) |
| 酉 (Yǒu) | Sep 8–Oct 7 | **Autumn** (Metal) |
| 戌 (Xū) | Oct 8–Nov 6 | **Autumn→Winter** (Earth transition) |
| 亥 (Hài) | Nov 7–Dec 6 | **Winter** (Water) |
| 子 (Zǐ) | Dec 7–Jan 5 | **Winter** (Water) |
| 丑 (Chǒu) | Jan 6–Feb 3 | **Winter→Spring** (Earth transition) |

### Step 4: Map All Characters to Ten Gods (十神)

Against the Day Master, every other stem and branch character takes on a **relational role**. These are the **Ten Gods** — they map life relationships onto the chart.

| Relationship | Positive (正) | Negative (偏) | Represents |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Produces me** (生我) | **正印** Direct Resource | **偏印** Indirect Resource | Mother, education, nurturing, support |
| **I produce** (我生) | **伤官** Hurting Officer | **食神** Eating God | Creativity, expression, children, talent |
| **Controls me** (克我) | **正官** Direct Officer | **七杀** Seven Kill | Career, authority, law, discipline |
| **I control** (我克) | **正财** Direct Wealth | **偏财** Indirect Wealth | Money, possessions, spouse (male), father |
| **Same as me** (同我) | **比肩** Friend | **劫财** Rob Wealth | Siblings, peers, competition, allies |

> **How to derive Ten Gods:** Use the Five Element cycle. If your Day Master is 甲 (Yang Wood):
> - 壬/癸 (Water) produces Wood → Resource (印)
> - 甲/乙 (Wood) is same as you → Peer (比劫)
> - 丙/丁 (Fire) is produced by Wood → Output (食伤)
> - 戊/己 (Earth) is controlled by Wood → Wealth (财)
> - 庚/辛 (Metal) controls Wood → Authority (官杀)

### Step 5: Check Hidden Stems (藏干)

Each Earthly Branch hides 1–3 Heavenly Stems inside. These represent **hidden potentials** — talents, influences, and support that aren't immediately visible.

| Branch | Hidden Stems |
|---|---|
| 子 (Zǐ) | 癸 |
| 丑 (Chǒu) | 癸, 辛, 己 |
| 寅 (Yín) | 甲, 丙, 戊 |
| 卯 (Mǎo) | 乙 |
| 辰 (Chén) | 乙, 戊, 癸 |
| 巳 (Sì) | 庚, 丙, 戊 |
| 午 (Wǔ) | 丁, 己 |
| 未 (Wèi) | 乙, 己, 丁 |
| 申 (Shēn) | 戊, 庚, 壬 |
| 酉 (Yǒu) | 辛 |
| 戌 (Xū) | 辛, 丁, 戊 |
| 亥 (Hài) | 壬, 甲 |

> **Practice tip:** When counting elemental strength, always count hidden stems at half weight (0.5 points each). They matter more in deep analysis and less in quick readings.

### Step 6: Score Day Master Strength (1–10)

Use the quantitative system from [Section 2](#2-day-master-strength-scoring-1-10-scale) below. This gives you a single number telling you how robust the person's core energy is.

### Step 7: Diagnose Special Pattern or Regular Pattern

Before applying standard balance logic, check if the chart qualifies as a **special pattern**:

- **Very strong DM (score 8–10):** Check for Zhuan Wang Ge (专旺格) — one element dominates ≥80% of the chart
- **Very weak DM (score 1–3):** Check for Cong Ge (从格) — DM is overwhelmingly weak with no support
- **Else (score 4–7):** Regular pattern — standard balance logic

Use the decision trees in [Section 5](#5-special-pattern-interpretation).

### Step 8: Determine Yong Shen (用神) and Ji Shen (忌神)

The **Yong Shen (Useful God)** is the single most important element for the chart — when it appears in Luck Pillars or Annual Pillars, good things happen. The **Ji Shen (Taboo God)** is the element that brings challenges.

| Pattern | Yong Shen | Ji Shen |
|---|---|---|
| **Regular — Strong DM** | Controls DM (官杀) or drains DM (食伤/财) | Supports DM (印/比劫) |
| **Regular — Weak DM** | Produces DM (印) or same as DM (比劫) | Controls/drains DM (官杀/财/食伤) |
| **Zhuan Wang** | DM's own element | Controls DM |
| **Cong Ge** | The dominant element | DM's own element |

Also check **climate adjustment (调候)** — if the chart is extremely cold/hot/dry/wet, the climate need may override pattern-based Yong Shen.

### Step 9: Read Luck Pillars (大运)

Luck Pillars are 10-year periods derived from the Month Pillar. They show the **macro-trend** of each decade of life.

- **Forward cycle (顺排):** Yang Male + Yin Female
- **Reverse cycle (逆排):** Yin Male + Yang Female
- **Starting age:** Count days to next/previous Solar Term ÷ 3

For each Luck Pillar, ask:
- Does the stem provide the Yong Shen? → Favorable decade
- Does the stem bring the Ji Shen? → Challenging decade
- Does the branch clash with the Day Branch? → Relationship issues
- Does the branch form a combination with natal branches? → Transformative events

### Step 10: Read Annual Pillars (流年)

Each year has its own Pillar. The Annual Pillar interacts with:
1. The **Natal Chart** (core foundation)
2. The **Current Luck Pillar** (10-year trend)

Key interactions to watch:
- **Clash (冲)** with any natal pillar → upheaval in that life area
- **Combination (合)** with natal pillar → merging of energies
- **Same as Yong Shen** → excellent year
- **Same as Ji Shen** → challenging year
- **Ben Ming Nian (本命年)** — year matches birth year branch → traditionally challenging

## 2. Day Master Strength Scoring (1–10 Scale)

This is a **quantitative system** for evaluating how strong the Day Master is in the chart. A score of 1 = extremely weak, 10 = overwhelmingly strong.

### 2.1 Component 1: Seasonal Power (60% weight — up to 6 points)

Use the **旺相休囚死** (Wàng Xiāng Xiū Qiú Sǐ) table to score the Day Master's element by season:

| Season | Wood | Fire | Earth | Metal | Water |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Spring** (寅/卯/辰) | 旺 (6) | 相 (4.8) | 死 (1.2) | 囚 (2.4) | 休 (3.6) |
| **Summer** (巳/午/未) | 休 (3.6) | 旺 (6) | 相 (4.8) | 死 (1.2) | 囚 (2.4) |
| **Autumn** (申/酉/戌) | 死 (1.2) | 囚 (2.4) | 休 (3.6) | 旺 (6) | 相 (4.8) |
| **Winter** (亥/子/丑) | 相 (4.8) | 死 (1.2) | 囚 (2.4) | 休 (3.6) | 旺 (6) |

**Numeric values:**
- **旺 (Wàng)** = 6.0 points — peak power, element rules the season
- **相 (Xiāng)** = 4.8 points — nourished, strong support
- **休 (Xiū)** = 3.6 points — resting, moderate
- **囚 (Qiú)** = 2.4 points — trapped, weak
- **死 (Sǐ)** = 1.2 points — dead, minimal

> **Special case — Earth transition months (辰/未/戌/丑):** The last 18 days of each season are ruled by Earth. For practical purposes, treat 辰 as Spring/Earth, 未 as Summer/Earth, 戌 as Autumn/Earth, 丑 as Winter/Earth. Earth gets 旺 (6.0) during its own transition periods.

### 2.2 Component 2: Same-Element Support (20% weight — up to 2 points)

Count every occurrence of the Day Master's **same element** in the chart (including hidden stems at half weight):

| Occurrences | Points |
|---|---|
| 0 | 0 |
| 1 | 0.5 |
| 2 | 1.0 |
| 3 | 1.5 |
| 4+ | 2.0 |

**Scoring example for a Wood Day Master:**
- 甲 in stem = 1 occurrence
- 卯 in branch (contains 乙) = 1 occurrence
- 寅 hidden (contains 甲) = 0.5 (hidden)
- **Total:** 2.5 → rounded to 3 occurrences → 1.5 points

### 2.3 Component 3: Producer Support (10% weight — up to 1 point)

Count occurrences of the element that **produces** the Day Master:

| Occurrences of Producer | Points |
|---|---|
| 0 | 0 |
| 1 | 0.3 |
| 2 | 0.5 |
| 3 | 0.8 |
| 4+ | 1.0 |

**For Wood DM:** Producer = Water (壬/癸/亥/子 + hidden)
**For Fire DM:** Producer = Wood (甲/乙/寅/卯 + hidden)
**For Earth DM:** Producer = Fire (丙/丁/巳/午 + hidden)
**For Metal DM:** Producer = Earth (戊/己/辰/戌/丑/未 + hidden)
**For Water DM:** Producer = Metal (庚/辛/申/酉 + hidden)

### 2.4 Component 4: Root Check (10% weight — up to 1 point)

Does the Day Master have a **root** — a branch containing the same element?

| Root Condition | Points |
|---|---|
| No root at all | 0 |
| Hidden root only (e.g., 甲 hidden in 寅) | 0.3 |
| Root in one branch | 0.5 |
| Root in two or more branches | 0.8 |
| Root in Month Branch specifically | +0.2 bonus |

> **Why this matters:** A stem without a root is like a tree without soil — its energy is floating and unreliable. A strong root makes the DM stable.

### 2.5 Calculating the Total Score

```
Total Score = Seasonal Power + Same-Element Support + Producer Support + Root Check

Range: 1.2 (min) to 10.0 (max)
```

### 2.6 Interpreting the Score

| Score | Classification | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **1.2 – 2.5** | Extremely Weak | DM has almost no support. Check for Cong Ge (从格). |
| **2.6 – 4.0** | Weak | DM is deficient. Needs producer (印) or peer (比劫) support. |
| **4.1 – 5.5** | Slightly Weak | DM has some support but is below average. Can benefit from mild reinforcement. |
| **5.6 – 6.5** | Balanced | DM is well-proportioned. The chart naturally balances itself. |
| **6.6 – 8.0** | Slightly Strong | DM has advantage. Needs some controlling (官杀) or draining (食伤/财). |
| **8.1 – 9.5** | Strong | DM is very strong. May approach Zhuan Wang. Needs clear controlling element. |
| **9.6 – 10.0** | Overwhelmingly Strong | DM dominates. Check for Zhuan Wang Ge (专旺格). |

### 2.7 Quick Reference: Pre-Scored Seasonal States

For a **quick estimate** without full calculation:

| Day Master Element | Spring | Summer | Autumn | Winter |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Wood** | Strong (7-9) | Weak (3-4) | Very Weak (1-3) | Moderate (5-6) |
| **Fire** | Moderate (5-6) | Strong (7-9) | Weak (3-4) | Very Weak (1-3) |
| **Earth** | Very Weak (1-3) | Moderate (5-6) | Weak (3-4) | Weak (3-4) |
| **Metal** | Weak (3-4) | Very Weak (1-3) | Strong (7-9) | Moderate (5-6) |
| **Water** | Weak (3-4) | Weak (3-4) | Moderate (5-6) | Strong (7-9) |

> **Important caveat:** These are seasonal baselines only. The full score from Sections 2.2–2.4 can shift the final number by up to ±4 points.

## 3. Ten Gods Interpretation

This section explains what **each Ten God** means when it appears in each pillar position, covering career, wealth, relationships, and health. Both positive and negative manifestations are given.

### 3.1 Overview Table

| Ten God | Chinese | Core Meaning | Element Relation to DM |
|---|---|---|---|
| **正印** (Direct Resource) | 正印 | Nurturing, education, support | Produces DM |
| **偏印** (Indirect Resource) | 偏印 | Unique talents, spirituality, non-traditional | Produces DM |
| **食神** (Eating God) | 食神 | Gentle creativity, enjoyment, skill | Produced by DM |
| **伤官** (Hurting Officer) | 伤官 | Bold creativity, rebellion, brilliance | Produced by DM |
| **正官** (Direct Officer) | 正官 | Discipline, career, order, authority | Controls DM |
| **七杀** (Seven Kill) | 七杀 | Pressure, challenges, power, risk | Controls DM |
| **正财** (Direct Wealth) | 正财 | Stable income, salary, savings | Controlled by DM |
| **偏财** (Indirect Wealth) | 偏财 | Windfall, investments, business | Controlled by DM |
| **比肩** (Friend) | 比肩 | Peers, siblings, cooperation | Same as DM |
| **劫财** (Rob Wealth) | 劫财 | Competition, rivalry, shared resources | Same as DM |

### 3.2 Ten Gods in Detail

---

#### 正印 (Direct Resource — 印星)

| Aspect | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| **Element** | Produces the Day Master |
| **Personality** | Nurturing, scholarly, supportive, conservative, trustworthy |
| **Career** | Education, academia, healthcare, government, counseling. Good for long-term stability. |
| **Wealth** | Indirect wealth channel — money comes through knowledge, reputation, position rather than direct business |
| **Relationships** | Strong mother figure. Nurturing partner. May be too protective or smothering. |
| **Health** | Generally good. Overabundance → laziness, dependence, sluggishness |
| **Positive** | Wisdom, patience, reliability, learning ability, good memory |
| **Negative** | Dependency, conservatism, resistance to change, over-cautiousness |
| **In Stems** | Prominent, visible influence. Education and support are important life themes. |
| **In Branches** | Background support. Hidden nurturance. Family or institutional backing. |

|---|---|
| **Element** | Produces the Day Master (same element as 正印, different polarity) |

| **In Stems** | Obvious unique talents or unusual life path |
| **In Branches** | Hidden eccentricities, spiritual interests developed later in life |

### 3.4 Ten God Balance Rules of Thumb

1. **Too many Resources (印过多):** Over-protected, dependent, lacks drive
2. **Too many Outputs (食伤过多):** Over-expressive, scattered energy, talks too much
3. **Too many Authorities (官杀过多):** Overwhelmed by pressure, stress, multiple bosses
4. **Too much Wealth (财过多):** Over-focused on money, materialism, but DM can't control it → financial loss
5. **Too many Peers (比劫过多):** Excessive competition, financial leakage, difficulty alone

> **Ideal balance:** A chart with **2–3 favorable Ten Gods** and **1 Tribulation element** is ideal. Extreme concentration of any single Ten God creates imbalance.

## 4. Elemental Balance Diagnosis

This section covers **climate diagnosis** (Cold/Hot/Dry/Wet) and how to find the **用神** (Yong Shen / Useful God) and **忌神** (Ji Shen / Taboo God).

### 4.1 The Four Climates

Every chart has a **temperature and moisture** quality that affects what element is truly needed — even overriding pattern logic in extreme cases.

| Climate | Chinese | Characteristics |
|---|---|---|
| **Cold (寒)** | 寒 | Winter-born; abundant Water (壬/癸/亥/子); Metal supporting Water |
| **Hot (热)** | 热 | Summer-born; abundant Fire (丙/丁/巳/午); Wood supporting Fire |
| **Dry (燥)** | 燥 | Abundant dry Earth (戌/未); lack of Water; Fire influence |
| **Wet (湿)** | 湿 | Abundant Water (亥/子) or wet Earth (辰/丑); lack of Fire |

### 4.2 Climate Diagnosis Method

#### Step 1: Assess Season

| Month Branch | Season | Baseline Climate |
|---|---|---|
| 寅 (Tiger) | Spring | Warming (cold→hot) |
| 卯 (Rabbit) | Spring | Warm, Wood |
| 辰 (Dragon) | Spring→Summer | Warm, **wet** (Water reservoir) |
| 巳 (Snake) | Summer | **Hot** |
| 午 (Horse) | Summer | **Peak hot** |
| 未 (Goat) | Summer→Autumn | **Hot**, **dry** (Fire reservoir) |
| 申 (Monkey) | Autumn | Cooling |
| 酉 (Rooster) | Autumn | Dry |
| 戌 (Dog) | Autumn→Winter | Cooling, **dry** (Metal/Fire reservoir) |
| 亥 (Pig) | Winter | **Cold** |
| 子 (Rat) | Winter | **Peak cold** |
| 丑 (Ox) | Winter→Spring | **Cold**, **wet** (Water reservoir) |

#### Step 2: Check Heavenly Stems for Climate Modifiers

- **丙/丁** (Fire stems) → makes chart Hotter
- **壬/癸** (Water stems) → makes chart Colder
- **戊/己** on 戌/未/辰 → Dry (戌/未) or Wet (辰)

#### Step 3: Check Earth Branches for Hidden Climates

| Branch | Climate Contribution |
|---|---|
| 辰 (Dragon) | Wet (Water reservoir — 癸 hidden) |
| 戌 (Dog) | Dry (Fire reservoir — 丁 hidden) |
| 丑 (Ox) | Wet (Water reservoir — 癸 hidden) |
| 未 (Goat) | Dry (Fire reservoir — 丁 hidden) |
| 亥/子 | Very Cold + Wet |
| 巳/午 | Very Hot + Dry |

#### Step 4: Synthesize Net Climate

```
Net Climate = Season + Fire/Water presence + Dry/Wet Earth presence

Examples:
- Winter (子/丑) + 壬水 + 亥水 = "Very Cold and Wet"
- Summer (午/未) + 丙火 + 未土 = "Very Hot and Dry"
- Spring (寅/卯) + 甲木 + 辰土 = "Warm and slightly Wet"
- Autumn (申/酉) + 庚金 + 戌土 = "Cool and Dry"
```

### 4.3 The 调候 (Diao Hou) Principle — Climate Adjustment

**调候 (Diao Hou) = Climate Regulation.** Every chart needs climate balance regardless of pattern type.

| Climate | Problem | Solution (调候用神) |
|---|---|---|
| **Too Cold** (过寒) | Stagnation, depression, freezing | Add **Fire** (丙/丁/午/巳) — warmth thaws |
| **Too Hot** (过热) | Agitation, burnout, volatility | Add **Water** (壬/癸/亥/子) — cooling soothes |
| **Too Dry** (过燥) | Rigidity, conflict, dryness | Add **Water** (moisture) |
| **Too Wet** (过湿) | Passivity, stagnation, heaviness | Add **Fire** (drying warmth) |

> **Critical rule:** Climate adjustment can **override** pattern-based Yong Shen. For example:
> - A Wood Zhuan Wang born in winter: Pattern says Yong Shen = 木/水, but climate says 火 (warmth) is urgently needed. **Climate wins** — 火 is primary Yong Shen, 木/水 are secondary.

### 4.4 How to Find Yong Shen (用神) and Ji Shen (忌神)

**用神 (Yong Shen)** = The single most important element for the chart. When it appears in Luck or Annual Pillars, it brings the best outcomes.

**忌神 (Ji Shen)** = The element that opposes the Yong Shen. When it appears, it brings challenges.

**喜神 (Xi Shen)** = Favorable elements that support the Yong Shen.

**闲神 (Xian Shen)** = Neutral elements — neither help nor harm significantly.

#### Decision Process

```
Step 1: Identify the Pattern
├── DM overwhelmingly STRONG → Check Zhuan Wang
├── DM overwhelmingly WEAK   → Check Cong Ge
└── In between (4-7)         → Regular Pattern

Step 2: Assess Climate
├── Extreme climate? → 调候用神 takes priority
└── Normal climate?  → Pattern logic applies

Step 3: Apply Pattern Logic

Regular Pattern:
├── DM Strong → Controlling element (克) = Yong Shen
│               Draining element (泄) = Secondary
└── DM Weak   → Producing element (生) = Yong Shen
                Same element (同) = Secondary

Zhuan Wang:
├── Yong Shen = DM's own element
├── Xi Shen   = Producer of DM's element
└── Ji Shen   = Controller of DM's element

Cong Ge:
├── Yong Shen = Dominant element
├── Xi Shen   = Producer of dominant element
└── Ji Shen   = DM's own element + its producer
```

#### Yong Shen Tables by Pattern

| Pattern | Yong Shen | Xi Shen | Ji Shen |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Regular (Strong DM)** | 官杀 (克DM) | 财/食伤 (泄/耗DM) | 印/比劫 (support DM) |
| **Regular (Weak DM)** | 印 (生DM) | 比劫 (同DM) | 官杀/财/食伤 |
| **Wood Zhuan Wang (曲格)** | 木 | 水 | 金 |
| **Fire Zhuan Wang (炎上格)** | 火 | 木 | 水 |
| **Earth Zhuan Wang (稼穑格)** | 土 | 火 | 木 |
| **Metal Zhuan Wang (从革格)** | 金 | 土 | 火 |
| **Water Zhuan Wang (润下格)** | 水 | 金 | 土 |
| **Cong Guan/Sha** | 官杀 element | Producer of 官杀 | 印/比劫 |
| **Cong Cai** | 财 element | 食伤 (produces 财) | 印/比劫 |
| **Cong Shi Shen** | 食伤 element | 财 (produced by 食伤) | 印/比劫 |

#### Climate-Adjusted Yong Shen (当气候极端时)

| Climate | 调候用神 | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| Extreme Cold | **丙 (Yang Fire)** — the sun | Universal warmth |

## 6. Luck Pillar Reading

### 6.1 Fundamentals

**Luck Pillars (大运, Da Yun)** are 10-year periods derived from the **Month Pillar** of the natal chart. They represent the macro-trend of each decade of life.

| Concept | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Direction** | Yang Male + Yin Female = FORWARD (顺排) |
| | Yin Male + Yang Female = REVERSE (逆排) |
| **Starting Age** | Count days to next/previous Solar Term ÷ 3 |
| **Number of Pillars** | 8 (covers ~80 years of life) |
| **How derived** | Forward/Reverse from Month Pillar, +1 stem & branch per pillar |

### 6.2 How to Read a Luck Pillar

For each Luck Pillar, analyze these four layers:

#### Layer 1: Stem vs. Yong Shen / Ji Shen

| Luck Stem | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **= Yong Shen** | Excellent decade — opportunities, growth, ease |
| **= Xi Shen** | Good decade — supportive energy |
| **= Ji Shen** | Challenging decade — obstacles, lessons |
| **= Xian Shen** | Neutral — depends on Annual Pillar interaction |

#### Layer 2: Branch Interaction with Natal Chart

| Branch Behavior | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Clashes (冲) with Day Branch** | Relationship/spouse challenges in this decade |
| **Clashes with Year Branch** | Family issues, moving, foundation shaken |
| **Combines (合) with a natal branch** | New alliances, transformations in that life area |
| **Forms Triple Combination (三合)** | Strong new energy enters the life |
| **Same as a natal branch** | Amplifies that pillar's energy |

#### Layer 3: Life Stage

| Luck Pillar | Approx Age | Life Stage Focus |
|---|---|---|
| LP1 | 0–10 or start+10 | Childhood, education foundation |
| LP2 | start+10 to +20 | Adolescence, young adulthood |
| LP3 | start+20 to +30 | Early career, marriage |
| LP4 | start+30 to +40 | Peak career, family building |
| LP5 | start+40 to +50 | Mid-life maturity, wisdom |
| LP6 | start+50 to +60 | Later career, mentoring |
| LP7 | start+60 to +70 | Retirement, reflection |
| LP8 | start+70 to +80 | Legacy, old age |

#### Layer 4: 5-Year Sub-Periods

Each 10-year Luck Pillar divides into two 5-year phases:

- **First 5 years:** Heavenly Stem dominates (more visible, faster changes)
- **Last 5 years:** Earthly Branch dominates (slower, deeper changes)

### 6.3 Reading Method Template

When reading a Luck Pillar, use this template:

```
Luck Pillar: [STEM-BRANCH] (Age X to Y)

1. STEM Analysis:
   - Stem element: [Wood/Fire/Earth/Metal/Water]
   - Relationship to Yong Shen: [Favorable/Unfavorable/Neutral]
   - Ten God of stem: [Resource/Output/Authority/Wealth/Peer]
   - Key opportunities this decade: [...]

2. BRANCH Analysis:
   - Branch element: [Wood/Fire/Earth/Metal/Water]
   - Interactions with natal chart:
     * Clashes with: [list any]
     * Combines with: [list any]
     * Same as: [list any]
   - Hidden stems: [list]
   - Key challenges this decade: [...]

3. Overall Judgment:
   - [Favorable/Mixed/Challenging] decade
   - The person should [action advice based on stem/branch energy]
```

### 6.4 Early vs. Late Luck

| Type | Starting Age | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Early Luck** | ≤ 6 years | Luck active from childhood. Person experiences life phases early. |
| **Normal Luck** | 7–13 years | Most common. Luck begins in adolescence/early adulthood. |
| **Late Luck** | ≥ 14 years | Childhood/teen years driven purely by natal chart. Success comes later in life. |

> 三命通会: "Those with early luck achieve early; those with late luck succeed late."

### 6.5 Transition Year (交运)

When one Luck Pillar ends and another begins, that transition year is called **交运 (Jiao Yun)**. It is considered unstable:
- Both ending and beginning Luck Pillars influence simultaneously
- Avoid major decisions (marriage, house purchase, career change)
- Events can be unpredictable

Natal Chart (八字)      ← Foundation / intrinsic nature
    ×
